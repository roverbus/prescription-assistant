# Objective


- Target Users: Professionals like advisors, consultants, or clinicians who manage long-term, high-stakes client relationships under ethical or - regulatory constraints.
- The Goal: Explore an AI capability that helps these professionals make better decisions and have better conversations without replacing their human judgment.
- The Role: You are the Product Manager at a B2B company (not direct-to-consumer) responsible for this opportunity.


# Personal Story:

As a patient I encountered two scenario personally that "Prior Authorization" either erode my trust in the clinician's prescription, or leads to delay in treatment and lowered my confidence in the clinic's management of my prescription:

## Case 1

A few years ago, I had bad acne, and I went to dermatologist for treatment; The doctor prescribed me some doxycillin for treatment and sent the prescription to CVS. However, I was told by the pharmacist that the medicine for 3 months were $500. I wasn't explained by either the doctor or the pharmacist of the "prior authorization". Out of the eager for treatment, I paid $500, but I was frastrated and thought the doctor should have provided alternative.

## Case 2

Last year, I was treating H.Pyleri, a digestive system desease. The doctor prescribed Pylera and sent to my pharmacy Capsule. However, Capsule told me the medicine would need prior authorization and would cost $720 without it. I called the doctor office to initiate the process, and I didn't receive information whether it was approved after 1 week. I eventually found a coupon online that I can use with Amazon Pharmacy, and I called my doctor to send the prescription to Amazon Pharmacy and I used coupon instead of the insurance to get the medicine.

# Initial Hypothesis:

1. When physicians prescribe medications to the patients, they often face challenges of "prior authorization", the process is often not well-understood by patients and involves lengthy back-and-forth involving physicians, payer (insurance provider) and patients. This leads to delays for patients to receive the treatment or pay hefty out-of-pocket costs or give up the treatment plan, which in turn leads to frustration or mistrust towards the physicians. 

2. Opportunity exist to utilize AI to solve the real-time misalignment between clinical intent and payer complexity before prescribing, by listening to clinical conversation or monitor real-time EHR inputs, providing live feedback in the prior authorization success predictions, and guide physician in having a meaningful conversation with patients regarding the process and options.

3. In addition, the current system also doesn't provide alternatives to physicians when the prior authorization risk is high, such as the coupon option.
